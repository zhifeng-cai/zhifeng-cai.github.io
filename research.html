Research

Working papers

  • The AI Skill TrapNew
    Paper HighlightAI raises short-run productivity but slows skill accumulation; a data externality leads workers to over-adopt, generating a persistent skill trap in which they become AI-dependent. While decentralized AI intensity falls monotonically with skill, the planner's optimal AI intensity is hump-shaped.
  • Generative AI and Data Quality: Implications for Productivity, Labor Displacement, and PolicyR&R, Review of Financial Studies
    Paper HighlightWill AI put us out of work? Not as alarming as it sounds: the usefulness of AI depends on how good the data is, and adoption of AI could itself depress that quality.
  • Financial Development and the Housing Kuznets CurveR&R, International Economic Review
    with Feng Dong and Haoning Sun
    Paper HighlightWe document that the house price-to-rent ratio first increases and then decreases with financial development. A simple model of financial frictions with a land sector can account for this pattern.
  • Labor Participation, Human Capital Accumulation, and the Business Cycle
    with Rosemary Kaiser
    Paper HighlightIn a quantitative life-cycle model with endogenous labor participation, we find that subsidies for job search among the elderly population can enhance overall welfare.
  • The Great Resignation and Optimal Unemployment Insurance
    with Jonathan Heathcote
    Paper CEPR WP HighlightOptimal design of unemployment insurance during the era of the Great Resignation, when many workers quit their jobs.
  • Self-fulfilling Resignation: Business-Cycle and Policy Implications
    Paper HighlightCan people quit their jobs because they expect others are doing the same? Yes — and this has new business-cycle and policy implications.
