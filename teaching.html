Teaching

I teach courses on macroeconomics across the curriculum at Rutgers — intermediate-level theory for undergraduates and advanced theory and topics for Ph.D. students. Course materials for currently enrolled students are posted on Canvas.

Undergraduate

  • Intermediate Macroeconomic Analysis (Econ 01:220:321)
    Rutgers University · taught Fall & Spring, most semesters
    A second course in macroeconomics. Topics: long-run growth, business cycles, the AD/AS framework, monetary and fiscal policy, financial crises, and long-run public-sector solvency. Textbook: Mishkin, Macroeconomics: Policy and Practice (2nd ed.).
  • The Chinese Economy
    Rutgers University
    A topical course on the macroeconomic, financial, and institutional features of the Chinese economy.

Graduate

  • Advanced Macroeconomic Theory
    Rutgers University, Ph.D. program
    Core Ph.D. macro sequence — dynamic general equilibrium, recursive methods, heterogeneous-agent models.
  • Topics in Macroeconomics
    Rutgers University, Ph.D. program
    Research-frontier topics in macro theory, drawn from my own and adjacent work: information frictions, financial frictions, labor-search, and the macroeconomics of data and AI.
